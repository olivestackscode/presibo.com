What Nigerians Need to Know About High Blood Pressure

By Osazuwa Osienem | Published: April 15, 2025

High blood pressure, also known as hypertension, is one of the most common—and most dangerous—health conditions in Nigeria. According to the Nigerian Heart Foundation, over 30% of adults are living with it, often without knowing.

What is High Blood Pressure?

It happens when the force of your blood against your artery walls is consistently too high. Over time, this can lead to heart attacks, strokes, kidney failure, and other serious problems.

What Causes It?

  • Poor diet (high salt intake, processed foods)
  • Lack of physical activity
  • Chronic stress
  • Excessive alcohol consumption
  • Family history and age

Symptoms to Watch Out For

Most people won’t feel anything until it’s serious, which is why it’s called the “silent killer.” Some may experience:

  • Severe headaches
  • Chest pain
  • Dizziness
  • Blurred vision
  • Shortness of breath
